# Guide

#### Maze

A Maze is an object with different audio modules. It is placed on the screen and is turned on&off like a track. The modules are: Gridsynth, Key, Rhythm and Sampler.

The position of the Maze point serves as a controller for playing the Gridsynth e.g. in combination with touch. It also determines the spatial sound (binaural/surround) of Rhythm and Sampler when Spatial is active.

All Mazes together form a Pattern. Mazes are unique to the Pattern and do not work across Patterns. They can be saved individually or as a Preset.

## Guide

### Into the Maze...

Even if it doesn't seem like it at first, you're entering a labyrinth here – a maze. Soniface has produced great depth and many functions over twelve years of development. It follows its own logic, which is motivated both technically and artistically. Exploring and playing an instrument is similar to this kind of journey. It's less about the result, rather more about the process…

### About Mazetools

Over 12 years ago, Stephan Kloß began the Maze project – a software core of instruments and multimedia interfaces. Since then, we've been dedicated to a very visual approach to shaping sound and music. We integrate motion tracking to turn the whole body into a controller and always understand sound spatially as well. We want to promote creativity, our path resembles a creative process, winding and at the same time open to ideas, art and technologies.Soniface is, strictly speaking, one Mazetool among several.

### Soniface - What to expect?

The basic structure follows a constantly running loop – a sequence in which further sequences of different instruments run. In this way, it's oriented towards the principles of generative music systems. This also applies to the close connection between interface, visual, and sound engine. Therefore, there are different modes, with and without visuals, as well as in different menu structures.

## Various Platforms

### Phone, Tablet, Desktop

Mazetools Soniface is available on iOS, Android, PC, Mac and Linux. The core of the app is the same, only in the connection of special interfaces, the platforms differ in part.

The way of use and performance acts differently on smartphone, tablet or desktop computer. We recommend a touch screen from 7", but it also works with mouse.

### Mazetools Soniface & Soniface Pro

Soniface and Soniface Pro are two different apps by Mazetools. Soniface is free and ad-free, because we want to inspire people to be creative. If you share this approach, feel free to support us, e.g. by purchasing the Pro version. This offers you unlimited instruments to add, scenes to create in pattern mode, e.g. for songs or performances. In addition, you have a visual mode for performance & VJing as well as unlimited internal audio recording (.wav, up to 7.1).

### Soniface Design

After starting the app, you can choose between two design modes. The mobile design is optimized for smartphones. The desktop design follows the original design and is recommended for tablets and desktops.

The choice of design by device is not a must. Try for yourself which one suits you. Within the app, interfaces and colors can be further customized.

### New Session

You start with a new session. It stands at the top of your projects' hierarchy. You save your session in "Projects" under "Save as" and assign a name.

Every session contains sets. These are unique snapshots of your composition, including the patterns (scenes) and the mazes (audio objects with instruments). Also pay attention to the help texts and tap on the three small gray dots next to them to move on to the next section.

### A Maze

This audio object contains four modules: the instruments Gridsynth, Rhythm and Sampler and the Key module for setting the key of a Maze. All Mazes are in a list, all active Mazes additionally on the screen.

Depending on the activity of the modules, tones are played by the movements, the sound and spatiality are changed. Modules can also be connected to each other.

### Add Instruments & Presets

Here you have modules to choose from, divided into instruments and presets. Selecting a type takes you to the submenu and gives you concrete suggestions.

Double-tap to add a new instrument and it opens. A new Maze is always created and listed in the Mazes list.

### A Pattern

All Mazes on the screen together form a Pattern, a scene of audio objects. To develop a composition, you can duplicate Patterns or add new/empty Patterns.

In Soniface Pro, this creates complex song sets, whereas Soniface Lite allows only one Pattern. The Patterns are displayed as a list, their respective position can be changed.

#### Create Pattern Presets

A Pattern can also be saved as a Preset. It contains all Mazes belonging to the Scene as well as all specific settings, such as Tempo, Connections, Motions and Visuals.

The Preset of a Pattern allows the storage of a scene independent of a Session and is an important basis for the future storage system.

### Sessions & Sets

Back to the beginning: You start and name your Session, and a Set is created in it. The Set is an image of your entire composition of Patterns, Mazes and Instruments, including all settings.

Each time you save, a new image of the Set is saved to the Set List. You can find the sets list with the name of the session in the local Mazetools Soniface folder (FAQ).

### Share Your Set

It is also possible to add a Set from one Session into a Set of another Session (Add+).

You can copy the Set file, paste it into the Soniface folder on another device and open it. If necessary, copy your samples as well and pay attention to the performance of the devices.

## The Grid

This is where the story of Mazetools Soniface begins: The Grid is the heart of Soniface and the central audiovisual element in the app's presentation and operation.

The Grid works like a stringed instrument. The shape and number of strings is variable. The colors tune the strings. In addition, the Grid visualizes the amplitudes of other instruments.

### A Maze on the Grid

The Grid Synth is one of 4 modules of a Maze and can be reached via the Maze Editor besides the Sampler, the Rhythm Module as well as the Color Module.

The position of a Maze on the Grid determines various functions, e.g. which tones are played (Grid Synth) or how the sound is arranged in the spatial (Sampler).

## Module

### Gridsynth Interface

The instruments are set in the editor. Under Synth you will find the Gridsynth. Visual and sound are combined in this instrument. The type of Grid determines the number of playable tones of the Gridsynth.

Sound modulation affects the shape of the Grid lines. The controllers of a Gridsynth also affect the sound of other active Gridsynths in other Mazes.

#### Gridsynth: Soundscape & Sequencer

The Gridsynth is played by changing the Maze Postition. Tones are played permanently. The number of notes played simultaneously is controlled by the Radius.

If the Trigger function is activated and the Maze is moved over the Grid, individual notes play and the Radius determines their length. The Gridsynth sequencer works in a similar way, containing several arpeggiators at the same time.

#### Gridsynth: LivePad Controller

A dynamic alternative to the input via the Sequencer is the LivePad . It displays the notes of the Gridsynth on the Grid, controls the filters and effects.

The LivePad can be active in addition to the Sequencer and is also influenced by the Sequencer speed.

### Key Interface

#### Set tones

The Interface sets the key and tonality of the instruments in the active Maze. The other Mazes also adopt these, unless they themselves have an active Key Interface.

This involves tuning the Gridsynth, Bass Synth, Melodic Sampler and Granular Synth to the same root, chord or scale (the pitch of a sample may need to be adjusted).

#### Sequencer for chord progressions

12 tones are arranged in a circle in 3 octaves. Around them is a circle - a sequencer, in which the selected tones and chords are placed simply by tapping.

In this way, chord progressions are created. Their sequence can be changed by drag & drop. The sequence tempo is used to set the length of the sequence.

#### Picker: Chords and Scales

In the Chord Picker, the root note is selected in the center. Chords and scales are selectable via the sliders. Scales and chords can also be added to the sequence via drag & drop.

There are 50 chords to choose from as well as 47 scales, mainly from the jazz area. The selection will be continuously expanded and we welcome suggestions.

#### Keyboard & MIDI

The selection of the tones can also be done via the internal keyboard, the keyboard of the device or a MIDI input e.g. a MIDI keyboard.

The change of the tones is done in real time. If a sequence is active, however, the sequence overwrites the changed tones again until it is stopped.

### Rhythm Interface

#### Bass & Drums

The Rhythm module offers two modes: Bass & Drums. They differ in sequencer and sound synthesis. In Bass Mode, there is a melody sequencer that aligns with the notes of the Chord Interface.

In Drum Mode, there are three step sequence rings for the elements bass drum (inside), snare (center) and hi-hat (outside).

#### Sequence-Pattern

The Rhythm Pattern function allows you to extend the Bass or Drum sequence up to 8 patterns. Patterns can be quickly expanded and modified by copy&paste.

The sequencer can follow a different quantization in each Rhythm Pattern. The division or multiplication of the sequence tempo is done globally for all Rhythm Patterns.

#### Sound & Envelopes

Both Rhythm Instruments work hybrid with samples and synthesizer, including drum library. The Bass Mode also includes a sample slicer function, which distributes the sample time over the sequence.

The sound of each drum element can be filtered and modeled separately. Curves for volume, pitch and filter are adjustable in the Envelopes menu.

#### LivePad : Drums & Bass

The LivePad offers a particularly dynamic input of the rhythm steps. This follows set quantization and offers its own modes of sequencing.

Envelope and sound as well as filters and effects are done via multitouch. The effects in the LivePad also control the controllers in the Maze Editor.

### Sampler Interface

#### Soundscapes & Melodies

The Sampler generates sounds based on external audio files. They become the basis for spatial noise spheres, looping sequences and granular soundscapes.

The In & Out points define the start and end time of the sample. A library brings many sounds and instruments. Custom samples are listed via the local Soniface folder under User.

#### Melodic Sampler & LivePad

In Melodic Mode, the Sampler Sequencer and LivePad become a harmonic pitch shifter. The sample is aligned with the fundamental in the Chord Interface.

You can adjust the pitch of the sample via the pitch steps. You can also combine the Granular Sampler with the Melodic function.

#### Granular Synthesis & Harmonic Arpeggiator

Granular synthesis can be used to create synth-like sounds from samples. The Harmonize function applies the selected notes of the Chord Interface to the grains.

This is done on the basis of an arpeggiator that runs through the scales and chords. Its speed determines whether it is perceived as a sound scape or a melody.

#### Effects

The FX Interface offers many effects to change the sound of samples and Granular Synths. The same Interface is also available in the Rhythm module for drums and basses.

Like all controllers inside the Maze Editor, Sampler effects can be automated. In this way, for example, a looping melody has a dynamic effect as it changes permanently.

## Automation

### Maze Controller

Each controller in the Maze Editor of a module can be automated (X: radius, Y: angle). By double-tapping on the controller, you enter the Automation Interface.

The simplest way is to draw a curve. There are also internal and external inputs to choose from to control the XY parameters of a controller.

#### Multi-Touch

Touch control is elementary in Soniface. Thus, by default, the Gridsynth can be controlled with multiple fingers around the Maze point. But all controllers are touch controllable.

The Multi-Touch Editor Interface allows to link the XY values of a controller to the Maze Point position or touch gestures (2-5 fingers).

#### Side-Chain

The controllers of a Maze can be automated by the amplitudes of the Rhythm elements. For example, a Bass Drum can lower or increase the volume of an instrument.

In this way, chains of Automations are created. They lead to constant changes in the perception of sound and musical dynamics.

#### MIDI In & Out

Maze controllers can be controlled by other apps and external devices via the MIDI Interfaces. Conversely, Maze controllers can control other apps.

The radius (X) and angle (Y) of a Maze controller are assigned to MIDI channel and controller respectively. For more information on MIDI In&Out, see Setup: MIDI.

#### Body Tracking

In this Interface you link movements of the body with the controllers of the instruments. In this way, you can play tones, control volumes, filters and effects with your hands and arms, for example.

45 points on the body send values whose range can be set individually. Tracking is done via Motion's Interface. In this front or selfie camera or external cameras are activated.

## Live

### Motion Tracking & Capturing

Soniface makes it possible to experience and create electronic music and sounds through movements of the body. This is often compared to the function of a theremin.

The body is recognized using machine learning models via the selfie or front camera and mapped as a skeleton model. In addition, Kinect or RealSense can be used for desktop and allow more spatial depth and width.

#### Motion Capture Editor

Activate Camera Tracking and, if necessary, press the Camera Toggle to change the camera of the device. Make sure there is enough light and align the device so that the camera images the body from a skeleton scheme.

Once this state is reached, XY controllers can be linked to the body via the Automation Interface. In order not to act permanently live while editing, movements can be recorded.

#### Captures & Tracking Presets

A recorded movement is listed in the Mocap menu and can be saved separately as a preset. Note that recording quickly generates large amounts of data.

The Motion Tracking Presets are sorted by body part, depending on which tracking connections they contain. The list is automatically expanded as new presets are saved.

#### Connections Hub: Controller Links

If Maze controllers are linked to other internal and external controllers via automation, they will be displayed in the Connections Hub. Tapping on them takes you directly to the link.

The Maze Hub shows all connections of the selected Maze, the Pattern Hub shows all connections of a Pattern.

#### Connections Controller Interface

In this Interface, all links are simulated, even if there is no connection to motion tracking, MIDI device or other controllers in the meantime.

Each link is indicated by a slider. When this is moved, it causes the corresponding behavior of the linked controller.

#### Master Effects

In this Interface, the entire sound of a pattern can be effected. This is useful for live improvisation, pattern transitions, and session recording. The effecting is temporary, because the controllers have a snapback function.

The hold button in the middle turns the effects on permanently, the controllers hold their position. Reverb and Echo each have an additional Filter & Feedback parameter controller.

#### Internal Recording

Unlike a DAW, the structure in Soniface does not follow a fixed timeline. It is therefore important that you record your sessions. Soniface records in WAV format (16bit).

The maximum recording time is 25min, after that the recording stops and saves automatically. You can find them in the local Soniface folder (FAQ) with the name of the session and the date.

### Spatial Audio

#### Sound Objects

Soniface works with spatial sound (3D audio). The Gridsynth, Sampler as well as Rhythm elements have spatializers. The position of a Maze on the screen determines whether the sound comes from the front, back, left or right.

In this way, the Mazes become sound sources in space on the horizontal plane. This becomes especially important when using surround sound.

#### Surround Sound System

You can connect a 5.1 or 7.1 speaker system via a sound card or HDMI. The arrangement follows the standard of the channels of 5.1 and 7.1 systems (see FAQ).

If a surround system is connected, the audio recording also records a surround WAV file in the respective channel number.

#### Binaural Experience

If you are using headphones, you can enable binaural audio in the setup menu. This way, the volume and filter change in relation to the distance from left and right as well as front and back.

The binaural algorithm in Soniface will be further developed in the future and is related to a general integration of VR/ AR/ MR application areas.

#### 3D Audio with Head Tracking

Increasingly, headphone models (e.g. Airpods) allow tracking of head movement and you can rotate in the sound scene like in a VR game.

Soniface supports this feature (iOS), but development is still in its early stages and will continue more.

## Visuals

#### Grid Visualization

The visuals in Soniface are created by combining the Grid, Chords, pulses of bass and drums, samples and motion tracking/captures.

The image is mirrored, multiplied, zoomed in and out, layered on top of each other by virtual cameras. Textures are arranged and placed on 3D bodies, for example.

#### Screen output

Via HDMI you can use a mobile device (iOS/ Android) to play a monitor/ beamer. On desktop (PC, Mac, Linux) up to 3 external monitors with separate visuals are possible.

Connect to the device and ensure power before starting the app. For desktop devices, the OS must be set to Extended Screens.

#### Modes & Textures

In the Screens menu you can set the mode of the visuals for the different screens. Under Main the current screen is displayed, under Out 1-3 the respective outputs of your device.

Square and Loop influence the texture. The options Character, Charback, Rotator and Hexagon determine the display of the textures in combination with a 3D figure.

#### Visuals Controller

The visuals can look different in each pattern. With the Copy& Paste function you can transfer all the settings, with Override Pattern, they are applied to all patterns.

In the Control and Character menus you can influence the display of the Grid and the 3D figure within the respective mode.

## Setup

### Audio

#### Session Settings

Tempo, volume and overall pitch are adjustable pattern-by-pattern. The Fix function defines a setting for the entire session.

Important parameters related to the audio representation of the session is the already described Binaural on& off function.

#### Ableton Link

Ableton Link allows you to sync the tempo to other Link apps over a local network. Make sure you are on the same network as the other Link apps/peers.

Sync shifts can be compensated for using the Reset Time function or the Offset fader. This can be the case e.g. with different latency settings or intensive calculations.

### Master Equalizer

The instruments in Soniface pass through a master bus. This contains a compressor and an equalizer. The Master EQ contains the following filters: Lowpass, Highpass, Bandpass, Peak, Notch, Lowshelf, Highshelf. It is pattern-wise changeable.

### System

#### Quality

In this menu, you define settings that directly influence the app performance. The Quality Settings affect the graphical performance: for older or entry-level devices, they should be low.

#### Latency

For the latency, it is the other way round: for high-end devices, it can be low, e.g. to reduce the delay between touch input or movement and the playback of a sound.

### Connections

#### Audiobus & Inter-App Audio (iOS)

The iOS versions of the Mazetools apps offer the possibility to route the audio output to a host app (Audiobus, AUM, Garage-Band).

Especially in combination with Ableton Link and MIDI, you can use Mazetools with other apps and control e.g. volume and tempo from the host app.

#### Interface Mode, Color & Zoom

You can customize the look of Soniface the way you like it best. The color scheme of the Interface, the size of the Grid, the zoom factor of the Interface.

The SimpleDesign UI mode is optimized for mobile devices, while desktops run with the classic design. Just decide for yourself - to turn it on and off you need to restart the app.

#### MIDI Input

Soniface is controllable external input devices or software. You can use a MIDI keyboard to control the Chord Interface and enter chords, bass synth and sampler are playable as MIDI instruments.

The Maze controllers are also controllable via MIDI controllers. The MIDI connections are displayed in the Connections Hub. When you save a preset of an instrument, the MIDI connection is also saved.

#### MIDI Output & Grid MIDI

The MIDI output of Soniface is especially interesting if you want to use motion tracking to convert your movements into MIDI notes and control parameters in your DAW, for example.

The Gridsynth can also output its notes via MIDI and thus play the sounds of other synths.
