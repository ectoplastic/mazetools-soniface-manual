# Introduction

### Welcome to Soniface Pro!

Thank you for your purchase! With the Pro version you get two essential features: the pattern mode for songs and the visual interface, for extended functions for visual design. You can find an overview of the features in the guide, tap on the three small dots to move on to the next section.

The basic structure follows a constantly running loop – a sequence in which further sequences of different instruments run. In this way, it's oriented towards the principles of generative music systems. This also applies to the close connection between interface, visual, and sound engine. Therefore, there are different modes, with and without visuals, as well as in different menu structures.

Every session begins with a point on the Grid called Maze. Each maze gives you access to the menus on one hand as well as the 4 sections: Grid Synth, Keys, Sampler and Rhythm.

Always remember to save your session, especially before you change setup settings. We wish you lots of joy!

#### The Grid: Synth, Visual & Interface

The Grid connects sound and visualization. The main instrument here is the gridsynth of a maze. Tap on the three small gray dots to learn more and move on to the next text section.

The basic structure follows a constantly running loop – a sequence in which further sequences of different instruments run. Therefore, you turn on instruments by turning up the volume.

The Gridsynth works with fixed notes, chords or scales of the key interface. Multiple mazes with active Gridsynth influence each other in sound and key.

The Grid is also in relation to the other instruments and their visualization. Rhythm and sampler have Grid modes, which set the pitch of both instruments in relation to the maze position on the Grid.

#### Pattern

All the Mazes on the screen together form the Pattern. They can be cloned and modified, allowing composition to evolve progressively.

The order of Patterns is variable. Each Pattern can vary in tempo, pitch, volume, Grid mode, and visual.

Tap, hold, and release the next Pattern icon to create a crossfade.

> *Also in: [Pattern](../06-pattern/)*

#### Maze

Each Maze offers a Gridsynth, a Sampler & Granular Synth and a Rhythm Module for Drums & Basses.

They are controlled via the Maze Editor or played in via the LivePad .

The position of the Maze affects the pitch of the Gridsynth and the spatiality of the Sampler.

> *Also in: [Mazes](../09-mazes/)*

#### Projects Save & Load

Projects are saved as a session. Press Save as... to name and save a new session

This will automatically create a .Set and a .Mazes file in the local Soniface folder with the session name

A session can contain any number of sets, which are displayed in a list in the Sets menu.
