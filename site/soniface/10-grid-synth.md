# Grid Synth

## The Grid: Synth, Visual & Interface

The Grid connects sound and visualization. The main instrument here is the gridsynth of a maze. Tap on the three small gray dots to learn more and move on to the next text section.

The basic structure follows a constantly running loop – a sequence in which further sequences of different instruments run. Therefore, you turn on instruments by turning up the volume.

The Gridsynth works with fixed notes, chords or scales of the key interface. Multiple mazes with active Gridsynth influence each other in sound and key.

The Grid is also in relation to the other instruments and their visualization. Rhythm and sampler have Grid modes, which set the pitch of both instruments in relation to the maze position on the Grid.

> *Also in: [Introduction](../01-introduction/) · [Visual](../07-visual/)*

## Gridsynth: Visual Synthesizer

In this instrument, the lines of the Grid and their shape act together with tones and timbres. The type of Grid determines the number of tones that can be played.

Assigning a root note, chord, or scale is done via the Chord selection.

Sound modulation affects the shape of the Grid lines.

Tip: The Wave controller controls the volume and frequency of the Gridsynth.

Even when Wave is not active, active controllers affect active Gridsynths in other Mazes.

## Wave: Volume & Frequencies

Radius (X): Gridsynth Volume

Angle (Y): Shift of the fundamental frequency along the circle in octaves

In the Gridsynth, the Wave control is important for the volume of the sound and brightness of the Grid.

Tip: Use automation by tapping the Wave icon and letting the volume get louder and softer in a sequence.

## Radius: Frequency, Number of Notes & Length

Radius (X): Number of notes played by the Gridsynth around the Maze point (Trigger off), length of notes Trigger on)

Angle (Y): The larger the angle, the softer the range of influence, longer release

When using Gridsynth Sequencer & Arpeggiator, the Radius refers to the note length.

Note: The more notes played, the more power the Gridsynth needs, also low frequencies can overlap, which can cause crackling.

## Key: Gridsynth Root Note

Radius (X): Type and number of notes played by the Gridsynth

Angle (Y): Switch between different chords whose individual notes are distributed on the Grid

The controller is a shortcut of the Key Interface. If the Key Interface is set manually, this controller is switched off.

## Panning: Gridsynth Stereo Field

Radius (X): Panning level

Angle (Y): Panning speed.

The stereo field expands the sound of the Gridsynth and makes it more variable.

## Vibrato: Gridsynth Pitch Oscillation

Radius (X): Vibrato level

Angle (Y): Vibrato Speed.

The vibrato effect changes the pitch vibration of the fundamental frequency.

## Lowpass: Gridsynth Filter

Radius (X): Frequency level

Angle (Y): Resonance level (Q factor).

Lowpass is a combination of lowpass, bandpass, and resonance filters, and modifies both the Gridsynth sound and the Sampler when GridSample is enabled.

## Highpass: Gridsynth Filter

Radius (X): Cutoff frequency

Angle (Y): Level of the resonance curve.

The Gridsynth effect filters out low frequencies.

Note: This control also affects other Mazes and should not be automated.

## Gridsynth Note Shift

Shifts the distribution of notes on the Grid.

## Hexagon Grid Shift

Changes the number of active Grid lines/ frequencies of the Gridsynth.

This changes its basic sound, as more or fewer lines/ frequencies/ notes are active.

## Setup Menu

It includes options for volume and tempo, audio quality, latency, and graphics settings such as zoom and quality.

Other settings include: Ableton Link for tempo synchronization, MIDI In & Out settings for linking MIDI devices and apps.

## Drone/ Trigger Switch

Off: The continuous Gridsynth (Drone) runs, defined by the Maze position and the Radius Controller.

On: The Gridsynth is played when the Maze position changes.

As soon as Trigger is active, the Gridsynth notes are only played when triggered by the movement of the Maze point or played by LivePad or Keyboard.

## Movement Recording

Allows recording of movements and changes of the Maze points on the Grid and controllers in the Interface.

During recording the red recording light flashes in the upper right corner. As long as it is lit, a recording is made by each touch.

The recordings appear on the right side of the Interface and can be deleted by clicking the cross next to it.

## Keep Maze

When a Pattern is cloned, all Mazes are duplicated. Turning on Keep does not duplicate the Maze. It remains the same and any action will affect the setting of the Maze in the other Pattern as well.

## Gridsynth: Wave Shape

Change the sound of the Gridsynth by drawing your own waveform.

Synthesizers can create sounds with complex waveforms. The waveform of a uniform periodic sound affects the timbre of the synthesizer.

Examples of basic periodic waveforms include sine, square, triangle, and sawtooth.

## Waveforms

Choose the basic periodic waveform: sine, square, triangle and sawtooth.

## Edit Waveform

Edit the waveform to change the basic sound of the Gridsynth.

## Random Waveform

A waveform is generated randomly.

## Invert Waveform

The waveform is inverted.

## Clear Waveform

Resets the waveform to the base value.

## Gridsynth Physics & Special Effects

The Interface refers to the connection of sound and Grid.

The Room controller is a master reverb effect and is also applied to the Rhythm Interface and Sampler (when enabled).

The Animation controller changes the position of the Maze on the screen.

## Room: Maze Reverb

Radius (X): Reverb Volume

Angle (Y): Reverb Length

Room is a kind of master effect. It affects the Gridsynth as well as Rhythm Interface and Sampler if the Room button is active within these interfaces.

Tip: It is advisable to use the Room controller only in one Maze per Pattern, as it affects all Mazes.

## Gridsynth Filter LFO

Radius (X): Amplitude

Angle (Y): Oscillation Frequency

The effect adds an additional oscillation to the Gridsynth.

Tip: Combine the LowPass effect in the Sound Interface of the Gridsynth.

## Gridsynth Tremolo

Radius (X): Effect Strength

Angle (Y): Speed

The Tremolo effect provides a Rhythmic oscillation of volume. Through the Grid this is directly visualized.

Depending on the Gridsynth Radius Controller it also influences the Gridsynth of other Mazes.

## Maze Postion Animation

Radius (X): Radius of the circle animation

Angle (Y): Speed & direction of the Maze point

The Maze point moves along a circle. This allows you to play a Maze's Gridsynth, use Grid Bass and Grid Sampler functions.

Tip: The rotation also affects the spatial sound of Sampler and Rhythm Interface if Spatial is enabled in these interfaces.

## Grid Magnet

Radius (X): Pitch Shifter/ Magnet Strength

Angle (Y): Pitch Shift, Stretch and Contract

The Magnet is a radical way to change the pitch of the gridsynth and sampler.

Tip: The effect of the Magnet interacts with the Radius Controller and also affects all other Mazes with active Gridsynth and Sampler.

## Grid Auto-Sequencer

Radius (X): Number of played Grid notes around the Maze Point

Angle (Y): Sequence direction on the Grid

The Sequencer Controller works in addition to the Sequencer Interface.

## Sampler Modul Volume

Radius (X): Sampler Volume

An audio file, a sample, can be used as the basis for the Gridsynth. For this, the Grid Sample function must be activated in the Sampler Interface.

The volume and pitch of the sample affect the sample-based Gridsynth.

## Gridsynth Note Range

Radius (X): Amount of played tones around the Maze point

Angle (Y): Falloff, the larger the angle, the softer the sphere of influence

While the use of the Gridsynth Sequencer, the Radius is related to the note length.

Note: The more notes are played, the more performance is required and the more notes overlap.

## Gridsynth: Rain Sequencer

The set notes and Arps play/trigger the individual Grid lines.

Entering makes the continuously playing Gridsynth inactive (can be switched back on if required).

The center sequencer ring refers to the position of the Maze point on the screen.

The envelope for note length and filter are set in the Envelope menu and are dependent on the Radius Controller (Synth/Physics).

## Gridsynth Sequencer

The set notes play/trigger the individual Grid lines.

Entering makes the continuously playing Gridsynth inactive (can be switched back on if required).

The center sequencer ring refers to the position of the Maze point on the screen.

## Gridsynth Note

Place notes in the circles by tapping/clicking.

The highlighted middle ring refers to the note/Grid line of the Maze position on the screen.

The rings inside and outside refer to the Grid lines left and right of the Maze point.

## Sequencer Quantization

Quantization refers to the possible number of notes within a sequence.

This value is also related to the sequence speed (Speed 1x).

## Play& Stop Sequencer

Play/Stop plays and stops the sequence.

The LivePad can be played in parallel and is not affected by Play & Stop.

## Sequence Speed

The sequence can be slower or faster in relation to the main tempo. The sequence phase is divided (left) or multiplied (right).

The normal state is in the middle position.

## Delete Sequence

Delete all notes in the sequence.

## Sequence Shifter

All notes in the sequence are shifted according to the quantization.

## Arpeggiator Menu

The Arpeggiator (Arp) plays the Gridsynth Lines automatically around the Maze position.

The activated Arp Controllers refer to the playing direction and speed.

The Contact Arp refers to the Note Sequencer. It will also be active when Trigger button is activated (Synth/ Physics).

## Drone/ Trigger Gridsynth on/off

On/off switch to enable/disable the continuous Gridsynth during the sequence.

When active, the value of the Radius Controller refers to the number of played frequencies of the permanent Gridsynth and to the length of the notes of the sequencer.

## Contact Arp

The controller becomes active when Trigger is activated (Sound/Physics Interface).

The Contact Arp allows you to move a Maze on the screen to play/trigger the Gridsynth.

## Circle Arp

Radius (X): Enlarges the Arp radius

Angle (Y): Sequence phase length/reverse sequence.

Triggers all notes around the Maze point.

## Spiral Arp

Radius (X): Enlarges the Arp radius

Angle (Y): Sequence phase length/reverse sequence.

Triggers notes around the Maze point in the shape of a spiral.

## Butterfly Arp

Radius (X): Enlarges the Arp radius

Angle (Y): Sequence phase length/reverse sequence.

Triggers notes on the left and right of the Maze point.

## Sword Arp

Radius (X): Enlarges the Arp radius

Angle (Y): Sequence phase length/reverse sequence.

Triggers notes around the Maze point simultaneously.

## Random Arp

Radius (X): Enlarges the Arp radius

Angle (Y): Sequence phase length/reverse sequence.

Triggers notes randomly around the Maze point.

## Bending

Deforms the flow of the Arpeggiator as it moves around the Maze point.

## Gridsynth Envelopes

Define the dynamics of the notes by changing the envelopes for volume (top) and timbre (bottom).

The envelope is related to the Wave, Radius and LowPass controllers of the Gridsynth Sound menus.

The Attack, Decay, Sustain and Release values can be animated by Loop Recording or the LivePad .

## Volume Curve

The volume is determined by the starting point (Attack), maximum intensity (Sustain), decay time (Decay) to silence (Release).

The length of the note is also related to the Radius Controller in the Gridsynth Sound and Physics Interface.

The values can be animated by Loop Recording or the LivePad .

## Filter Curve

The timbre of each Gridsynth note is filtered vertically and applies the ASDR principle: Starting Point (Attack), Maximum Intensity (Sustain), Decay to Silence (Release).

The filter of the note is also related to the LowPass Controller in the Gridsynth Sound and Physics Interface.

The values can be animated by Loop Recording or the LivePad .

## Sequencer Effects

Disables/enables the influence of the Gridsynth Sound and Physics controllers on the sequencer/Arpeggiator.

## LivePad Input: Gridsynth

Live input of notes and parameters on area of screen.

Each input is repeated in a sequence: Impulse enters notes, Envelope adjusts dynamics, Filter controls timbre and Effects controls texture.

Loop speed is variable, input is Rhythmically quantized (see Maze Editor/ Rhythm/ Quantization).

Tip: X- and Y-axis can be linked to other input tools via Connection.

## Gridsynth Impulse

X-axis: Chord/ Scale of Chord Interface

Y-axis: Octave

Tap or swipe on the screen to play and loop the Maze's Gridsynth live.

Each interaction overwrites the previous input.

## Gridsynth Envelope

X-axis: ADSR curve

Y-axis: Amplitude

Interaction with the envelope changes the dynamics of the note. Its character also depends on the settings in the Sound menu (Radius) and in the Envelope menu (Volume, Filter).

## Gridsynth Filter

X-axis: Low Pass Frequency

Multi Touch Rotation: Filter Resonance

Input via Multi Touch generates a Filter Peak.

The interaction animates the LowPass Controller in the Editor.

## Gridsynth Effects

X-axis: Vibrato Speed

Y-axis: Vibrato Volume

The Vibrato Controller in the Sound menu of the Gridsynth is animated by the input.

## Weird Mode

Simultaneous control of impulses, envelope, filter and effects.

Tip: delete unloved tracks individually in the respective menu by Clear and keep the rest.

## Sequence duration

The speed of each interaction loop can be halved or multiplied in relation to the main loop.

Inputs already made are now played back faster or slower in relation.

> *Also in: [Sampler](../12-sampler/) · [Rhythm](../13-rhythm/)*

The Live Hub is a listing of all LivePad on top of each other. These are played simultaneously via touch or mouse input. The interface is still in beta state and will be further developed in the following versions.

## Sequencer Presets

The presets contain different sequences and envelopes of the Gridsynth.

Loading a preset overwrites the sequencer setting of the current Gridsynth (no undo).

## Save Sequencer Preset

Save a custom preset and name it.

The preset will be saved to the local Soniface folder and can be shared this way (FAQ).

## Add Sequencer Preset

Load a preset into the current Gridsynth Sequencer menu.

Presets contain different settings and sequences that can be freely changed.

Loading the preset overwrites the current settings.
